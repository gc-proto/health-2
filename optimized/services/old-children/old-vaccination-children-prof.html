Giving parents the proper information

As health professionals, it is important to give parents proper information about their children's vaccinations. Make sure you connect with each patient in order to maintain trust and keep the lines of communication open. Every parent needs different information from their health professional.

Parents will look to you for answers to their questions about immunization. You can prepare yourself by referring to the resources below. A successful visit might be where parents agree to get their child vaccinated that day, or schedule for another day.

If a parent refuses to vaccinate their child

If a parent refuses to vaccinate their child, keep communication open for future discussions about immunization. As a health professional, it is not recommended that you exclude children from your practice when their parents do not want to be immunized. It can put the child at risk for other health problems.

  • Be sure parents are aware of what vaccine-preventable diseases are and what symptoms can be expected.
  • Remind parents that they should call before they bring their sick child into the office, clinic or emergency department. This is so appropriate measures can be taken to protect others.
  • Be sure parents are aware of the school or daycare policies in their province or territory for unimmunized children.

Resources for health professionals

  • Canadian Immunization Guide: A resource on immunization for health professionals, vaccine program decision-makers and other Canadian stakeholders with an interest in immunization. A subscribe feature is available for automatic updates.
  • The National Advisory Committee on Immunization (NACI): A resource where health professionals can find immunization statements, recommendations and updates.
  • Vaccine Safety: A resource for health professionals on vaccine safety, including how to report an unusual vaccine event.
  • Travel Vaccines: A resource for health professionals to access information on vaccines a child may need when travelling.
    • Canadian Immunization Guide: Immunization of Travellers
    • Additional Online vaccination advice for travellers
    • Committee to Advise on Tropical Medicine and Travel (CATMAT) - Statements and Recommendations.
  • Vaccine-Preventable Diseases: A resource for the public and health professionals on signs, symptoms, causes, treatment, prevention, and surveillance of vaccine-preventable diseases.
  • Immunization Communication Tool for Immunizers (PDF) (British Columbia): A tool to help health professionals answer difficult questions from their patients about immunization.
  • Immunization Competencies for Health Professionals: A resource and online education program for health professionals, aimed at promoting safe and competent immunization practices. This is to protect against vaccine-preventable diseases through high vaccination coverage rates.
  • National Vaccine Storage and Handling Guidelines for Immunization Providers (2007): A resource for health professionals to learn about ensuring efficient vaccine storage and handling in your practice or public health program.
  • Publications and Reports: A collection of immunization policy reports, guidelines and summaries.

Resources for your patients or clients

  • CANImmunize App: An app for health professionals to keep track of vaccinations and receive alerts about disease outbreaks in their area.
  • A Parent's Guide to Vaccination: A free guide for parents to learn about immunizing their children.
  • Caring for Kids: Information for parents from Canada's Paediatricians. Information includes illnesses and infections, vaccinations for children, assessing immunization information on the internet, and Your Child's Best Shot book.
  • Immunize Canada: A website hosted by national non-governmental, professional, health, consumer, government and private sector organizations interested in immunization in Canada. Information can be found for parents, health professionals and specific groups, including resources for pain management during immunization.
  • Provincial and Territorial websites and immunization schedules: A list of websites for health professionals to access immunization content from across the country, including the latest immunization schedules.
